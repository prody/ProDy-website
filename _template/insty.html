Interactions and Stability

InSty is a module for characterizing the interactions and/or stability of proteins and their complexes/assemblies. The tool detects seven types of non-covalent interactions (hydrogen bonds, salt bridges, hydrophobic, pi-stacking, pi-cation, ionic repulsion, and disulfide bonds) within a protein structure and between proteins in complexes.

Features

InSty is designed to automate the analyses of protein structures (single structures or collections of structures from the PDB), or conformers from molecular dynamics trajectories. It detects the contacts relevant to protein stability and dynamics, using multiple criteria including geometric features (distances and angles). The code is flexible and allows the user to adapt the analysis to the system or protein region of interest.

InSty provides a suite of functions for generating raw data (including files for further analyses and visualization. It takes advantage of the ProDy framework to interpret the observation in the context of protein dynamics.

You can use InSty to

  • Identifying different types of non-covalent interactions within proteins or complexes
  • Identifying residues with a high propensity to associate with others
  • Identifying residues critical to stability and/or dynamics
  • Visualizing the probabilistic occurrence or time evolution of specific interactions
  • Comparing the interactions in different conformations or mutants
