Water Bridge Finder

WatFinder detects protein-water interactions (water bridges or water-mediated associationsor water clusters) relevant to protein stability and dynamics.

WatFinder can analyze single protein structures (PDB files), collections of structures (PDB ensembles), or conformers from molecular dynamics trajectories to detect water molecules that form water bridges within protein structures. It uses multiple criteria, including geometric features (distances and angles). The code is flexible and allows the user to tune the data analysis appropriately to the analyzed system.

WatFinder provides a suite of functions for generating raw data and outputs for statistical analyses and visualization tools, and takes advantage of the ProDy framework for interpreting the observation in the context of protein dynamics.

Features

You can use WatFinder for

  • Identifying water molecules in contact with the protein
  • Detecting pairs of residues linked by water bridges
  • Identifying the location of water clusters
  • Generating histograms displaying the frequency of occurrence of specific water bridges
  • Identifying residues with high propensity to associate with water molecules
